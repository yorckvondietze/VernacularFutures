About the climate scenarios

Present uses TMYx 2011–2025 files, Typical Meteorological Year files built from the most recent 15-year satellite+reanalysis record.

2050 and 2080 are morphed EPW files generated with the Future Weather Generator (FWG v4.1) (Rodrigues et al., 2023), implementing the Belcher et al. (2005) morphing methodology.

Morphing applies ensemble-mean climate change deltas from ~27 CMIP6 models under SSP5-8.5 (high-emission scenario) to the present-day TMYx file. Reference periods:

  • Present: 2011–2025 (TMYx observed)
  • 2050 horizon: 2036–2065 centred mean
  • 2080 horizon: 2066–2095 centred mean

Baseline for delta calculation: 1985–2014 (CMIP6 historical reference, centre ≈ 2000). Variables morphed: dry-bulb temperature, dew point, solar radiation, wind speed, atmospheric pressure. Precipitation is not morphed.

Methodological caveat, baseline mismatch: The FWG deltas represent warming from the 1985–2014 baseline (centre ≈ 2000). Applied onto TMYx 2011–2025 files (centre ≈ 2018), the morphed outputs effectively represent conditions roughly 18 years beyond their nominal labels:

  • "2050" label → effectively ≈2068 conditions
  • "2080" label → effectively ≈2098 conditions

This is conservative labelling: projections are likely warmer than the nominal year implies. Rodrigues et al. (2023) explicitly note this limitation. Results are reported under the FWG nominal labels for methodological transparency; findings should be read as representing at least 2050/2080 severity.

Belcher et al. (2005) Rodrigues et al., Building and Environment 246, 2023

MorphSHIFT is an empirical–quantitative correlation study testing which passive strategies in historical vernacular buildings remain valid under projected future climate and which require adaptation. It pairs this with a simulation tool that bridges description and prescription.
_pipeline

The pipeline

Each case study is anchored to the nearest representative EPW / TMYx weather station. Present-day files (TMYx 2011–2025) are morphed to 2050 and 2080 horizons using CMIP6 deltas under SSP5-8.5, generated with the Future Weather Generator (FWG), a Java-based tool implementing the Belcher et al. (2005) morphing methodology (Rodrigues et al., 2023) with bilinear interpolation. Precipitation is intentionally not morphed.

Architectural metrics are extracted from DXF drawings: wall thermal mass as floor-plan polygons extruded to eave height (C = Σ V × VHC), window-to-wall ratio, wall thickness via the perimeter–area formula, compactness ψ (clamped at 1.0), eave / rake depths and others. Each building reduces to a binary tag matrix matched against its EPW climate signature.

Correlations are inferred with logistic regression, treating country and altitude zone as confound controls because cultural and climate variables co-vary geographically. A two-zone unconditioned Grasshopper / Honeybee model (inhabited zone + attic buffer) is then simulated in EnergyPlus against present and morphed EPWs. This is the mechanistic step that lets correlations become defensible prescriptions.

Why simulation is mandatory

Observing that vernacular buildings correlate with their climate is descriptive. Recommending those features for new construction is prescriptive. Crossing that line on correlation alone is the naturalistic fallacy. Simulation licenses the jump and exposes the one variable a climate analogue can never fix: solar geometry is set by latitude, not climate, so a future-Cfa Bavarian site still receives Bavarian sun angles, not the sun of a present-day Cfa site further south.

Limitations

  • Naturalistic fallacy: correlation cannot by itself justify prescription; simulation is required and remains partial.
  • Incomplete corpus: geometry metrics exist for a subset of buildings; coordinates and EPW assignment are missing for others; Köppen classification and simulation outputs are not yet written back for most rows. live counts shown above
  • Precipitation: not morphed and unreliable for classification; future Köppen uses present-day precipitation to avoid rounding-driven misclassification.
  • Recent-warming bias in TMYx: 2011–2025 normals already embed warming, so some stations no longer classify as Dfb even where 1961–1990 normals would. Significant, not an error.
